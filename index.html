Information About These Numbers - Based on a Monthly Review

  • * Current Customers = Fill in the quantity of customers you currently have. If this is the first time filling this in, the suggestion is to calculate the last 12-months customer total -> the next time you review this, the amount should be increasing.
  • * Customer Retention Rate % = Calculate -> Retained Customers divided by Current Customers times by 100% Example: 99 retained customers ￼110 current customers x 100% = 90%
  • * Retained Customers = Customers that stayed with the business during the month
  • * Number of New Leads = How many new leads/prospects contacted you during the month?
  • * Conversion Rate % = Before you can fill this in, you need to establish the business goal -> is it calls, sales, sign-ups, downloads, form submissions *Ask yourself: How do I know I have converted a customer? -> then Calculate -> Number of Conversions based on business goals divided by Number of New Leads/Prospects times by 100% Example: 34 sales ￼new leads ￼ 100% = 15.3%
  • * New Customers = How many new cusomers you aquired in the month based on the conversion rate (business goal you decided on)
  • * Total Number of Customers = As the calculation shows this is your total amount of customers including retained + new.
  • * Number of Transactions = In the month how many invoices did you generate?
  • * Average Transaction Value = Calculate -> Total Sales $ divided by Total Number of Transactions
  • * Sales How much money have you made in the month = YAY!
  • * Cost of Sales = What total costs did you have to pay to generate these sales? Can you reduce it?
  • * Gross Profit = The money you generated after paying your costs.
  • * Operating Expenses = What are the total business operating costs? Can you reduce it?
  • * Net Profit = This is the final amount your business has generated AFTER Cost of Sales and Operating Expenses. This amount should be growing every time you review it.
